# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Process Engineers at Genentech Oceanside MSAT, primarily in Buildings 311 and 312. They open this tool mid-shift to locate a link, form, SOP, or equipment spec — speed and findability are non-negotiable. The equally important audience is the new hire or intern on their first week: they should be able to open this repository, scan the sidebar, and orient to the full PE landscape without asking anyone for a tour.

## Product Purpose

A single internal repository that organizes all upstream and downstream PE documents, spreadsheets, equipment assets, and resources for Oceanside MSAT. Success means a new process engineer can get fully oriented — and an experienced one can find anything — without falling back on personal bookmarks, asking a colleague, or emailing around for a link.

## Positioning

The one place every PE at Oceanside MSAT trusts to be current, complete, and immediately navigable — whether it's day one or year five.

## Brand Personality

Clear, professional, approachable. The tool reflects the team's rigor without being cold. An intern should feel welcomed by it; a senior engineer should feel it respects their time. Voice: direct and precise in labels, never jargon-heavy or bureaucratic, never casual enough to feel informal.

## Anti-references

Old intranet feel — anything that reads like a 2005 company portal: dense link tables, Times New Roman, Windows XP blues, zero visual breathing room. The tool should feel current without trying to be a startup SaaS dashboard.

## Design Principles

**First glance should orient.** The sidebar taxonomy and panel headers must tell a new engineer exactly where to begin, before they click anything. No hidden structure.

**Hierarchy carries the map.** Labels, groupings, and visual weight do the navigational work — users should never need to hold a mental model in their heads to use the tool.

**Approachable without being casual.** Friendly enough that an intern doesn't feel intimidated; rigorous enough that a veteran engineer trusts it. The tone is a well-run lab notebook, not a wiki or a ticket board.

**Precision over decoration.** Every visual choice earns its place by aiding comprehension or navigation. Ornament that doesn't help a user find something faster should be cut.

**Inert until needed, instant when called.** Search, sidebar, and links are the primary affordances. Everything else — transitions, hover states, panel animations — exists to confirm action, not to draw attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. All body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against its background; large text and UI components ≥ 3:1. Keyboard navigable (sidebar tabs, accordions, search). Reduced-motion alternative for panel fade and accordion animations. Color is never the sole differentiator for any state.
